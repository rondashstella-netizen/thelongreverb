Imprint of record

Signal Tower Press

A publishing imprint for books, witness archives, visual artifacts, listening work, and systems built where literature, sound, memory, and machine culture meet.

Continuity lock: Signal Tower Press is the canonical public imprint. “Signal House Press” and “Static Temple Press” are superseded names and are not current public branding.
Publishing field

Artifacts with traceable origin.

Signal Tower Press treats publishing as more than release. Each work carries editorial authority, rights boundaries, visual continuity, source records, and a defined relationship to the larger Long Reverb world.

Archive

Witness and cultural signal

Essays, interviews, ledgers, photographs, listening records, and recovered materials are published only after permissions and context are resolved.

Operating principles

The press keeps the authority visible.

  • Ron Dash Stella retains final approval over manuscripts, editions, public claims, and release state.
  • Draft, staged, experimental, and canonical assets remain explicitly separated.
  • No public page represents an unreleased work as purchasable or complete.
  • Protected spatial-audio methods and local production systems remain outside public publishing files.
  • Every release should connect craft, provenance, audience, and a viable path back to the work.

The imprint is not decoration. It is the chain of custody between the work, the reader, and the world that receives it.
